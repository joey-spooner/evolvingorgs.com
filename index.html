Evolving Orgs

How organizations evolve in the age of AI

AI is not just another tool. It changes how organizations decide, coordinate, learn, and operate. This is where leaders figure out what to do about it.

Read the Thinking Join the Email List

The Problem

AI is not a tool problem. It's an organizational problem.

Most AI adoption efforts treat the technology as a productivity layer on top of existing systems — faster reports, better search, smarter assistants. That's the easy part.

The harder part is what AI does to the rest of the organization: how decisions get made, how teams are structured, how coordination happens, how humans and machines divide work. These are not software questions. They're management questions. And most organizations aren't ready for them.

The Insight

Most organizations are trying to use AI inside outdated management systems.

The operating models most organizations run today were designed for a different era — one where information was scarce, decisions moved slowly, and coordination required layers of management.

AI accelerates everything. But if the underlying system isn't designed for speed, autonomy, and continuous adaptation, acceleration just creates more pressure on structures that were already struggling.

The organizations that will thrive aren't the ones that deploy the most AI tools. They're the ones that redesign how they operate around AI.

The Work

Redesigning organizations for the AI era.

Evolving Orgs explores the specific questions leaders are wrestling with: How do you redesign decision systems when AI can process more information faster than any human? How do you structure teams when the boundaries between human and machine work are blurring? How do you lead when the organization is changing faster than your management model?

The work here is practical, direct, and grounded in how organizations actually function — not how they appear on org charts.
