Quick diagnostic

Is your company affected by the EU AI Act?

The Act's high-risk obligations take effect in August 2026. Answer five questions to get a quick read on your exposure.

1. Does your company operate in the EU, or offer products and services to EU-based customers or employees?

2. Do you use AI to make decisions about employees — hiring, performance evaluation, task assignment, or work scheduling?

3. Does AI in your products or services affect access to credit, insurance, healthcare, or other essential services?

4. Does your company use AI in education, safety-critical systems, public services, or law enforcement contexts?

5. Do you build products that integrate large AI models (such as GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, or similar) and sell or deploy them to customers?

This is a rough indicator, not legal advice. The Act's full scope depends on your specific systems and use cases. Read the official overview →

Compliance readiness is an operating model question.

Clear AI ownership, documented oversight, and defined accountability don't just satisfy regulators — they're what makes AI work reliably in the first place. If that structure isn't in place yet, August 2026 is a useful deadline to build toward.
